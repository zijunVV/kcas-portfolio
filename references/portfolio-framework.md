# KCAS Portfolio Framework

## Core Principle

KCAS Portfolio does not judge assets from scratch. It consumes KCAS Core outputs and asks:

```text
What happens to the portfolio if this asset is owned, increased, reduced, or replaced?
```

Always separate:

- Asset-level quality from KCAS Core.
- Portfolio-level role and risk from KCAS Portfolio.
- New capital opportunity cost from both.

## Required KCAS Core Inputs

For each holding and candidate, collect:

- Asset Quality Score.
- Investment Attractiveness Score.
- Final Allocation Score.
- Knowledge Capital Analysis.
- Compounding Potential.
- Reflexivity.
- Primary exposure.
- Portfolio role, if available.

If unavailable, mark as missing and state what must be researched.

## Portfolio Diagnosis Ratings

Use `Low`, `Moderate`, or `High`.

### Concentration

- `Low`: no single asset or correlated cluster dominates.
- `Moderate`: one or two assets or clusters are meaningful but survivable.
- `High`: one asset, theme, factor, or sector can drive portfolio outcome.

### Diversification

- `Low`: too few independent return engines.
- `Moderate`: some diversification, but meaningful overlap remains.
- `High`: multiple independent knowledge capital and cash-flow engines.

### Factor Exposure

Evaluate growth, value, momentum, quality, cyclicality, duration, liquidity, and private-market risk.

### Knowledge Capital Exposure

Evaluate concentration in similar knowledge moats:

- Data moat.
- Workflow moat.
- Network moat.
- Talent moat.
- Learning loop.
- Ecosystem moat.

### Reflexivity Exposure

High reflexivity can compound upside but also amplify downside if narrative, talent, capital, or customer momentum reverses.

### Compounding Exposure

Reward portfolios with multiple durable compounding engines, but penalize if all compounding depends on one macro theme.

## Exposure Map

Report percent exposure for:

- AI Infrastructure.
- AI Software.
- Enterprise Software.
- Semiconductors.
- Cloud.
- Healthcare.
- Consumer.
- Industrial.
- Energy.
- Creator Economy.
- Other.
- Cash.
- Index Funds.

If an asset has multiple exposures, split exposure only when evidence supports it. Otherwise use primary exposure.

## Portfolio Scores

Use 0-10.

### Portfolio Quality Score

Weighted average of asset quality scores by current or proposed weight. Penalize if the portfolio is dominated by assets with missing core evaluations.

### Portfolio Resilience Score

Evaluate:

- Cash buffer.
- Diversification.
- Valuation risk.
- Macro sensitivity.
- Liquidity.
- Drawdown survivability.
- Independence of return drivers.

### Portfolio Compounding Score

Weighted average of compounding potential, adjusted for overlap. A portfolio with three different compounding engines should score higher than one dependent on a single AI infrastructure chain.

### Portfolio Concentration Score

Score high when concentration is healthy and intentional; score low when concentration is accidental or overlapping. This score is not simply "less concentration is better." A focused portfolio can score well if risks are understood and exposures are not redundant.

## Opportunity Cost Ranking

Always rank:

- Current candidates.
- Cash.
- Index funds.
- Alternative opportunities.

The top rank should be the highest-conviction use of new capital today after considering existing holdings.

## Allocation Recommendations

Use:

- `Increase`: improves portfolio quality, compounding, or opportunity cost without unacceptable concentration.
- `Maintain`: asset remains useful but additional capital is not clearly superior.
- `Reduce`: exposure is too large, valuation risk is high, or overlap is excessive.
- `Exit`: thesis broken, role unclear, or opportunity cost unacceptable.

Recommendations must explain portfolio-level effect:

- Does it improve quality?
- Does it reduce or increase concentration?
- Does it diversify knowledge capital?
- Does it improve compounding?
- Is it better than cash, indexes, or alternatives?

## Final Recommendation

Choose exactly one:

- `Allocate More`
- `Maintain`
- `Wait`
- `Reduce`
- `Avoid`

`Allocate More` means the next dollar has a clear destination. `Wait` means cash or more research is preferable today. `Reduce` means risk or opportunity cost argues for trimming. `Avoid` means candidates or portfolio action are unattractive.
